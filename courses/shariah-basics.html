Short Shariah (Fiqh Basics)

Practical Islamic Jurisprudence for your daily life and worship.

Level: Practical Duration: 3-6 Months Focus: Compliance & Ethics

Course Overview: Mastering the Path

Shariah is often misunderstood globally, but at its heart, it is the 'path' that leads to a balanced and ethical life. Our Short Shariah & Fiqh Basics Course at Usman Ibne Affan Online Quran Academy provides a clear, concise, and authentic introduction to Islamic Jurisprudence (Fiqh). This course is designed for busy individuals who want to ensure their daily acts of worship and worldly transactions are in accordance with the Divine Decree.

Knowledge of Fiqh is essential because worship without knowledge can lead to sincere but incorrect practices. Our curriculum focuses on the 'Fard al-Ayn' (individual obligation) knowledge that every Muslim must possess to lead a life of 'Ihsan' (excellence). We break down complex legal concepts into practical guidelines that are easy to remember and apply.

Primary Learning Outcomes

We focus on the most relevant areas of Islamic law for a modern Muslim:

  • Fiqh of Taharah (Purification): Detailed rules of Wudu, Ghusl, and dealing with physical and ritual impurities.
  • Fiqh of Salah (Prayer): Mastering the pillars, conditions, and recommended acts of prayer, including the rules of the traveler and the sick.
  • Zakat and Charity: Calculating Zakat accurately and understanding the ethical priorities of giving in Islam.
  • Sawm (Fasting): The jurisprudence of Ramadan, what breaks the fast, and the rules of Fidya and Kaffarah.
  • Islamic Ethics and Transactions: Understanding the basics of Halal earnings, prohibited interest (Riba), and fair trade in the modern world.
  • Family and Social Law: Basic rights and responsibilities within a marriage, and towards parents and offspring.

Our Teaching Methodology

We aim for total clarity and practical application:

Case-Study Based Learning: We present real-life scenarios to discuss how Fiqh rules apply, making the abstract legal codes relatable to your daily experiences.

Authentic Sources: Our teachers reference established schools of thought (Madahab) with a focus on ease and mainstream scholarly consensus (Ijma) ensuring you are on firm ground.

Digital Handouts: We provide summarized cheat sheets and diagrams (e.g., of Wudu steps or Zakat calculations) that you can refer to at any time.

Open Q&A Environment: We believe no question is too small or too embarrassing. Our teachers provide a respectful space for clarifying even the most private matters of faith and practice.
